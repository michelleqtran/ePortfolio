Comments on The Economist's "Clean energy's dirty secret..."

Clean energy's dirty secret: Wind and solar power are disrupting electricity systems | The Economist

This article brought up a point I did not previously consider. That is, the marginal cost of renewable energy is negligible. Based on economics, if the marginal cost is negligible than energy providers have every incentive to pursue renewable energy because they can only profit from it. However, the equipment needed to impliment renewable energy is expensive. Furthermore, the article notes that traditional power plants are necessary to provide electricity when renewable sources of energy (such as wind or sunlight) are scarce. This further supports the arguement for clean energy. Furthermore, it was encouraging to me that the article states that new technology will drive down the cost of clean energy, in part through digitalization.
