Hi, I’m Michelle

Scholar + Leader + Scientist + Enviromentalist

In the classroom

I am currently a sophomore Computer Science and Mathematics double major with Leadership Studies and Economics minors in the Honors College at the University of Houston. For my time in college, I hope to combine my interests in computer science and mathematics by pursuing a data science focus. I am finding opportunities to learn data analytics and data visualization by reaching out to university researchers outside of the classroom.

To learn more about my academic experience, visit The Scholar page.

Outside the classroom

Other experiences outside of the classroom have also shaped my interests and goals. As a freshman I joined the Bonner Leaders Program, a service-learning organization whose mission is to alleviate poverty in the Houston Third Ward community. My experience in the program has made me more conscious of the many issues that affect low-income households. Combined with my study abroad experience in the Galapagos Islands—where I learned firsthand the importance of environmental conservation—my mission became the search for a way to achieve clean energy without the added cost that would otherwise fall upon the impoverished.

To learn more about my leadership experience, visit The Leader page. To learn more about my research experience, visit The Scientist page.

In the future

For summer 2017, I will be continue learning more about data analytics while helping to conduct research in carbon capture and storage at the Department of Energy’s National Energy Technology Laboratories in Morgantown, West Virginia through the Mickey Leland Energy Fellowship. In the future, I plan to attend graduate school before applying my efforts towards affordable clean energy initiatives.

To learn more about my thoughts on energy and environment news, visit The Environmentalist page.
