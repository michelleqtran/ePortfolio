Hi, I’m Michelle

Scholar + Leader + Scientist + Enviromentalist

I am currently a sophomore Computer Science and Mathematics double major with Leadership Studies and Economics minors in the Honors College at the University of Houston. During my time in college, I have combined my love for computer science and mathematics, developing a passion for data science. I have found numerous of opportunities to learn data analytics and data visualization by reaching out to university researchers outside of the classroom.

Other experiences outside of the classroom have also shaped my interests and goals. As a freshman I joined the Bonner Leaders Program, a service-learning organization whose mission is to alleviate poverty in the Houston Third Ward community. My experience in the program has made me more conscious of the many issues that affect low-income households. Combined with my study abroad experience in the Galapagos Islands—where I learned firsthand the importance of environmental conservation—my mission became the search for a way to achieve clean energy without the added cost that would otherwise fall upon the impoverished.

For summer 2017, I will be using my skills in data analytics and visualization to help conduct research in carbon capture and storage at the Department of Energy’s National Energy Technology Laboratories in Morgantown, West Virginia. In the future, I plan to attend graduate school for data science with a focus on Big Data before applying my skills towards affordable clean energy initiatives.
